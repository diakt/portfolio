    "Patrolling and controlling"

Well, hello. My name is Gavin. I'm a bit of a mutt, but I'd say I'm from Washington state. I’m a mathematics major at Columbia graduating in the summer of '22. I'm more of an analyst than an algebraist, more of a probabilist than an analyst, and more of a fool than a probabilist. I rowed for a while and fought wildland fire for a while. My favorite authors are Bulgakov, Pynchon, Kafka, Solzhenitsyn, and McCarthy, although maybe not in that order.

I was born on Vancouver Island, Canada, and moved to Vancouver, Washington when I was four years old. I went back to Vancouver Island for high school, then worked/taught for a year in Canberra, Australia. After that, I worked in the diabetes research group at B.C. Children’s Hospital in Vancouver, Canada for a year before heading to Columbia University. A lot of Vancouvers.

I row at Columbia and won a national championship on the lightweight rowing team. I’ve worked irregularly throughout school and held a couple of on-campus leadership positions, though nothing too noteworthy. Near the end of my junior year, the pandemic started and I decided to drop out wildland firefight out of PatRick’s base in Ellensburg, Washington, which is about a hundred miles ESE of Seattle. I enjoyed the work immensely. After the fire season ended, I worked as a contact tracer, and later interned at Zwift in a research role the summer before returning to Columbia to study for my senior year.

I’m deeply interested in environmental hazards and risk management. Wildfire will worsen by several orders of magnitude over the next fifty years in the Pacific Northwest and Russia. I enjoy using statistical approaches in combination with significant assumption hunting to examine risk in a systems context, and this passion has developed in tandem with my love for mathematics and computer science.

If you have something to spare, I’ve attached a donation link in the bottom-right footer to contribute to Alexandra Elbakyan’s sci-hub project, which strives to ensure free open-source access to scientific research. It is difficult to overstate the importance of sci-hub in the fight to challenge current paradigms of pay-to-play knowledge access, and Elbakyan’s work has been sensational. I sincerely recommend visiting sci-hub and seeing what it has to offer.
